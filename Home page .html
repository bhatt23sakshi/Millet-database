Home page

Millets are highly varied groups of small- seeded grasses, small-seeded plant species which can grow extreme weather conditions and they may have numerous health benefits, such as helping lower your blood sugar and cholesterol levels. They are well adapted to grow under low soil fertility, low moisture, and hot environmental conditions and for its germination the minimum temperature required is 8-10°C . Millets are of value especially in semiarid regions because of their short growing season and higher productivity under heat and drought conditions.

Millets are grown in tropical as well as sub-tropical upto an altitude of 2,100m. The advantages of harvesting millet crop , such as production and brief season period, capable of withstanding dry and extreme weather conditions of 64°C with low rainfall of 350–400 mm annually , and poor irrigation facilities, have gained popularity, leading it to be a drought-resistant plants . Millets are crops with unique features that grow under robust and competing conditions .

Millet is a staple food. Millets are eaten in various parts of the world, such as Russia, Poland, and China, in different forms (porridge or congee). Sudanese pancakes made from pulverized millet are well known. Millet flour is consumed as porridge and served with mixed vegetables and a meat sauce in Senegal, whereas in Pakistan, it is eaten as a cuisine in the form of chapati, most of millet crops are native of India and in India, it is consumed as khichdi, idly, Weaning Food, and chapati. Commercial meals made from millet include noodles, millet juice, and puffed millet.

Millets are good sources of energy and highly nutritious . They provide protein, fatty acids, minerals, vitamins, dietary fibre and polyphenols. Typical millet protein contains high quantity of essential amino acids especially the sulphur containing amino acids (methionine and cysteine). Processing millet by milling removes the bran and germ layers that are rich in fibre and phytochemicals, causing significant loss. The millets are source of antioxidants, such as phenolic acids and glycated flavonoids and millets are rich source of bioactive compounds such as dietary fibre , flavonoids and phytochemicals etc . Millet foods are characterized to be potential prebiotic which means it supports good bacteria in your digestive system .
