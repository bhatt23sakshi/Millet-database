Flavonoids

Finger millet is a highly beneficial source of flavonoid compounds, which have been identified in both the grain and leaves of the millet. While finger millet flavonoids are primarily present in a soluble form, little is known about their respective quantities, and more research is needed in this area. Esterified forms of flavonoids have also been reported in finger millet, which are distinct from those found in other millets like kodo millet. The major flavonoids found in finger millet are quercetin, catechin, gallocatechin, epicatechin, and epigallocatechin, along with proanthocyanidins or condensed tannins, which are oligomeric or polymeric flavonoids. Procyanidin B1 and B2 have been reported as the major dimers in the grain. These phenolic compounds are known for their antioxidative, antiproliferative, anti-allergic, and anti-inflammatory properties. However, there is currently no information on the specific structural representations of these phenolic compounds in finger millet, and more research is needed to characterize these compounds and their bioactivities. Such information will be important for predicting the health benefits of finger millet consumption based on the phenolic compounds involved.

Functions and applications of flavonoids

Flavonoids have been associated with numerous health-promoting effects, such as antioxidative, anti-inflammatory, anti-mutagenic, and anti-carcinogenic properties. These properties, coupled with their capacity to modulate key cellular enzyme functions, make flavonoids an indispensable component in a variety of applications in the nutraceutical, pharmaceutical, medicinal, and cosmetic industries. Flavonoids act in plants as antioxidants, antimicrobials, photoreceptors, visual attractors, feeding repellents, and for light screening. The antioxidant activity of flavonoids is of particular interest due to their ability to reduce free radical formation and scavenge free radicals. Flavonoids have also been found to have anticancer properties by inhibiting the activities of certain P450 isozymes responsible for producing procarcinogens, as well as helping to produce metabolizing enzymes that detoxify carcinogens and eliminate them from the body.
