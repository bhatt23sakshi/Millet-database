Ferulic Acid

Ferulic acid is an organic compound with the chemical formula (CH3O)HOC6H3CH=CHCO2H. It belongs to the group of hydroxycinnamic acids and is classified as a phenolic phytochemical. The name "ferulic" is derived from the giant fennel plant genus Ferula, from which it was originally isolated. Ferulic acid and its esters are widely distributed in plants, where they are covalently bonded to polysaccharides such as hemicellulose and pectin. Due to its antioxidant properties, ferulic acid is used in some skin care products to help reduce inflammation, signs of aging, and even out skin tone.

Chemical Structure

IUPAC Name

(E)-3-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid

Molecular Formula

C10H10O4

Properties of Ferulic Acid

  • Molar mass of ferulic acid is 194.18 g/mol .
  • Appearance of ferulic acid is Crystalline powder .
  • Melting point of ferulic acid is 168 to 172 °C (334 to 342 °F; 441 to 445 K).
  • Solubility in water of ferulic acid is 0.78 g/L .
  • Acidity (pKa) of ferulic acid is 4.61 .

Curative Benefits of Ferulic Acid

  • Fades Away Wrinkles:

    • Ferulic acid, a phenolic compound found in plants, exhibits strong antioxidant properties. It is commonly used in skincare products to protect the skin from the damaging effects of environmental stressors, such as pollutants and radiation, which can contribute to the development of wrinkles and other signs of aging.

  • Lowers Inflammation:

    • Ferulic acid has been found to possess both antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect the skin from oxidative damage caused by environmental pollutants and radiation. This protection can help prevent the formation of wrinkles and other signs of aging. Additionally, these properties can also reduce inflammation in the skin, which can lead to clogged pores and breakouts. As a result, the use of ferulic acid in skincare products may help improve overall skin health.

  • Treats Melasma:

    • Melasma is a chronic pigmentary skin condition in which melanocytes become hyperactive in response to sunlight and infrared radiation. Ferulic acid is known for its ability to protect the skin against infrared radiation and has been studied for its potential to improve melasma.
