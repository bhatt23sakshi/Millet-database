p-Coumaric acid

p-Coumaric acid is a type of coumaric acid that is found in edible plants as a component of lignins and tannins, and is a hydroxy derivative of cinnamic acid. It can be found in various foods including carrots, tomatoes, and cereals. [1] . p-Coumaric acid is a phenolic acid that can be found in bound form in cereals, and can be released through various processes such as fermentation, thermal treatment, pasteurization, and freezing. [4] . It has been shown to possess various beneficial properties such as antioxidant [1] , anti-inflammatory [2] , and anticancer [3] , reduces lipid peroxidation, cholesterol oxidation, and low-density lipoprotein [1] . [5] Khare et al. have even quantified the presence of 1.38 ppm of p-coumaric acid in kodo millets, which has been shown to help reduce obesity.

Structure of p-Coumaric acid

IUPAC name

(2E)-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid

Chemical formula

C9H8O3

Health benefits

  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Anticancer
  • Anti-obesity
  • Reduces lipid peroxidation
  • Cholesterol oxidation
  • LDP
