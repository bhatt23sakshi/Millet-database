Tocols

Tocols, which include tocopherols and tocotrienols, are a group of compounds commonly found in both animal-based (such as dairy) and plant-based foods (like oils and nuts), where they are associated with lipids. Tocopherols are methylated phenols with a saturated side chain, while tocotrienols possess an unsaturated isoprenoid side chain. Besides their well-known vitamin E and antioxidant activities, tocols have been found to exhibit other biological activities of interest. During the processing and storage of food, chemical reactions can occur that degrade food constituents, leading to the accumulation of compounds that may compromise the sensory and nutritional quality, safety, and shelf-life of food. The oxidative deterioration of lipid-rich foods, in particular, can be protected by endogenous or added antioxidants, especially tocols. Therefore, tocols can play a critical role in safeguarding food against oxidative deterioration during processing and storage, particularly in lipid-rich foodstuffs. However, the minor constituents of oils and fats derived from different sources (plants, animals, and microorganisms) may differ significantly from one another. The methods used to extract oils and fats, as well as industrial processing techniques such as degumming, refining, bleaching, and deodorization of vegetable oils, can also impact bioactive constituents. Moreover, post-processing treatments such as heating, frying, cooking, or storage may also lead to the degradation of essential bioactive constituents, notably tocols.

Health Benefits

  • Improving fracture healing

    A study conducted on rats with osteoporosis that had reached menopause found that tocotrienols were more effective in strengthening bones and promoting faster healing of bone fractures compared to other vitamin-E supplements.

  • Neuro-protective effects

    • Studies conducted on humans indicate that tocotrienols have the ability to readily cross the blood-brain barrier, enabling them to potentially enhance brain health and cognitive function.

  • Anticancer effects, and increased overall health.

    • Scientific research indicates that tocotrienols possess several health benefits for humans and may particularly exhibit anti-cancer properties.

  • Reducing risk of cardiovascular disease and lowering cholesterol levels.

    • Studies suggest that tocotrienols may aid in reducing the accumulation of arterial plaque and lowering cholesterol levels.
