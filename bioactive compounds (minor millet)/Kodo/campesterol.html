Campesterol

Campesterol is a common plant sterol found abundantly in nature, such as in cereals, nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, beans, and legumes. [1] [2] Its structure is similar to cholesterol, and like cholesterol, it can be metabolized by intestinal bacteria, leading to cholesterol-lowering benefits. [3] There are various experimental evidences that support the fact that campesterol has a chemopreventive effect against cancers such as breast, lung, and prostate cancers, performing anticarcinogenic activity. [4] [5] [6] [7] The concentration of campesterol in raw and germinated kodo millet flour was determined to be 0.31% and 2.60%, respectively. [8]

Kodo millet also contains various phenolic compounds such as methyl vanillate, arachidonic amide, N (3,5-dinitropyridin, 2yl) L-aspartic acid ester, N-propyl 9,12,15-octadecatrienoate, pregan-20-one, 2, hydroxyl-5,6 epoxy, 15-methyl, hexadecanoic acid, 9, octadecenoic acid, butyl 6,9,12,15, octadecatetraenoate, hexadecanoic acid, methyl ester, methyl 10-trans, 12-cis octadecadienoate, stigmasterol, C-sitosterol, and pregnenolone. [8] These compounds are identified to have various health benefits such as antimicrobial, nematicide, anticancer, antieczemic, carcinogenic, diuretic, antiasthma, amino glycoside antibiotic, antioxidant, anticoronary, hepta-protective, hypocholesterolemic,

Structure of Campesterol

IUPAC name

(3S,8S,9S,10R,13R,14S,17R)-17-[(2R,5R)- 5,6-dimethylheptan-2-yl]-10,13-dimethyl2,3,4,7,8,9,11,12,14,15,16,17-dodecahydro1H-cyclopenta[a]phenanthren-3-ol

Chemical formula

C28H48O

Health benefits

  • Lowers cholesterol , shows anticarcinogenic
  • Chemopreventive effect
