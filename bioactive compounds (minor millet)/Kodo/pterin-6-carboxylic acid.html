Pterin-6- carboxylic acid

Folic acid (vitamin B9) is a water-soluble vitamin that plays an essential role in DNA synthesis, cell division, and growth [1]. However, under ultraviolet radiation, folic acid can degrade into 6-formylpterin, which is further oxidized to pterin-6-carboxylic acid and generates reactive oxygen species (ROS) [2]. Phenols are known for their antioxidant properties and can scavenge ROS to prevent oxidative damage. Sharma et al. [3] reported that raw and germinated kodo millet flour contained 0.5 and 15.84% concentration of phenols, respectively, suggesting that germination can significantly increase the antioxidant capacity of kodo millets.

Structure of Pterin-6- carboxylic acid

IUPAC name

2-Amino-4-oxo-3h-pteridine-6-carboxylic acid

Chemical formula

C7H5N5O3

Health benefit

Antitumor
